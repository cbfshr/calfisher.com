Quadroid
Fall 2015, Group Project
Source: github.com/cbfshr/Quadroid
With a little bit of reverse engineering, anything is possible!
Quadroid is a unique team project that consists of a Syma X11 Quadcopter, an Arduino board, and a Nexus 7 Android tablet. The ambitious end goal of the project was to be able to control the quadcopter with the Nexus 7 tablet.
The way we went about it was like this: we first needed to reverse-engineer the default RF controller for the quadcopter. Next, we needed to be able to emulate electrical signals that would normally be determined by the resistance of the joysticks. We connected wires to the Arduino board and directly soldered the other end of the wires to the controller's circuit board where the joysticks previously were.

The picture to the left shows the wiring between the Arduino and controller circuit board.
Joystick Control
On the other end of the spectrum, the Android application needed to do two things: emulate two joysticks and send the input information to the Arduino (using the Arduino Mega ADK). (Pictured to the left)

From there, the Arduino could send the correct signals to the controller, which would then control the quadcopter.

Once we got it working 100%, we were able to add a second flight mode to the Android application which allowed us to fly the quadcopter by controlling the roll and pitch with theaccelerometer values on the Android tablet. (Pictured to the right)
Accelerometer Control
The project worked surprisingly well, and we went above and beyond the minimum requirements by implementing such a complex, reverse-engineered system.
